Large Pitchfork huntsman
Large Huntsmen are inhabitants of Loran who have begun their transformation into Werewolves. Although they still retain some degree of their humanity, their appearance has become much more bestial and they started to avoid their former comrades , indicating the onset of the scourge of beasts.

Blood Cultist
The cult of Blood is a religious organization founded in Loran and is also its ruling body. They worship blood and refer to it as the purest form of Essence. When the beasts took Lower Loran, the cult barricaded the door, abbandoned the citizens outside to their fate.

Blood Cultist with Torch

Leech Collector
From antiquity, leeches were used to treat all kind of ailments. When the plague of blood and beast struck Loran, the demand of leech in medical treatment quadrupple and thus leech collectors become a common sight. Poor and old women often work as leech collectors, using their own blood to attracts the blood-thirsty vermins. But every now and then, the city guards would find a pale, bloodless body of a leech collector after they were sucked dry. On rare, wicked occasion, the leeches make their way deep into the victim's body through their orifices, reanimate it to seek newer prey.

Leech
From antiquity, leeches were used to treat all kind of ailments. The doctors believe leeches would suck out the bad blood, an would apply leeches to the inside of their patients' throat or even make the patients swallow the leeches whole. Little do the doctors nor the patients know, the leeches, once inside a human body, multiply, slowly turning the patient into a walking nest of leeches. If the victim start to vomit live leeches, then it is too late.

Mosquito Swarm
The humid condition of the sewers are perfect for these winged pest to lay their eggs. They typically emerge all at once in massive swarms. It is the talk of the town when the Dungeon mosquitoes are out. Because these little, ferocious blood suckers are relentless. They do not stop. They only leave when all that left of their victim is a bloodless, lifeless, shriveled husk.
